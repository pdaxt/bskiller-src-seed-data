Jack Clark's Import AI #447 highlights a joint MIT/WashU/UCLA paper titled 'Some Simple Economics of AGI' that argues AGI won't cause the economic apocalypse that doomers predict  -  but it won't create the utopia that accelerationists promise either. The paper models AGI as a 'general-purpose technology' like electricity or the internet, which historically take 20-30 years to fully diffuse through the economy. Key finding: even with AGI, labor market disruption peaks at ~15% displacement over a decade, not the 50%+ that viral Twitter threads claim. The paper is refreshingly grounded in actual economic modeling rather than vibes.
