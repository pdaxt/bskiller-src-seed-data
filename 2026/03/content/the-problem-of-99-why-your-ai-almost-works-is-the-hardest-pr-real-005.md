Ethan Mollick's latest analysis digs into a pattern every AI engineer knows but nobody talks about: AI that works 95-99% of the time is often MORE dangerous than AI that works 50% of the time. Why? Because 50% failure is obvious and gets caught. 99% failure is invisible until it ruins something important. He cites data showing power users use 7x more 'thinking' tokens than casual users  -  suggesting the people who depend on AI most are the ones hitting the failure modes hardest. With 900M weekly ChatGPT users, even a 1% failure rate means 9 million failures per week.
