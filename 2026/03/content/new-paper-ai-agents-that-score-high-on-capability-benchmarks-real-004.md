A February 2026 paper on arXiv (2602.16666) tested 14 AI models across 12 reliability metrics and found that capability gains don't translate to reliability gains. Models that ace benchmarks still hallucinate, drop context, and fail silently in production. The key finding: the correlation between standard benchmark scores and real-world reliability was only 0.31  -  essentially no relationship. This matters because every AI vendor pitches benchmark scores as proof their model is 'production-ready.' This paper shows that's fundamentally misleading.

Receipts:
• arXiv paper: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.16666
• 14 models tested including GPT-5, Claude 4.5, Gemini 2.0, Llama 3.1
• 12 reliability dimensions: hallucination rate, context faithfulness, instruction following, etc.
• Correlation coefficient 0.31 between capability benchmarks and reliability metrics
